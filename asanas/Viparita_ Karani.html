VIPARITA KARANI 

                Viparita Karani is often called Legs-up-the-Wall Pose, but viparita actually means “inverted,” and karani means “in action.” We can 
                interpret that to mean that the pose inverts the typical actions that happen in our bodies when we sit and stand. There are many 
                benefits to inverting the actions in your body. Here are a few. When you put your legs up the wall with your pelvis elevated on a folded
                blanket, lymph and other fluids that can lead to swollen ankles, tired knees, and congested pelvic organs flow into the lower belly; 
                this refreshes the legs and the reproductive area. This is healthy at any point in your reproductive life cycle.

                Steps:
                Lie down on a flat surface. Position yourself in a way that your tailbone is pointed at the base of the wall.
                Shimmy your hips as close as possible to the wall, then start walking your feet up the wall until your body forms an L shape.
                Make adjustments so that the posture feels relaxing, use a pillow under your head or a towel at the base of your back.

                Benefits:
                The Viparita Karani mudra helps relax your feet and provides benefits if you have leg pain.
                The posture helps improve sleep.
                It is known to reduce swelling and oedema of the ankles and sleep caused by inactivity like sitting or standing for a long time. 
                The Viparita Karani mudra for sleep yoga asana benefits people with cramped legs.
                Doing this yoga before sleep gives your torso and the back of your legs the stretch they need.
                It is one of the best yoga poses for sleep as it gives relief from a mild ache in the back.
                This yoga before sleep helps stretch out the back of your neck.
